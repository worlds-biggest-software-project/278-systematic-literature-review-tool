## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **PRISMA 2020 (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses)** — gold-standard reporting guideline for systematic reviews; platforms increasingly automate PRISMA flowchart generation
- **Cochrane Handbook for Systematic Reviews of Interventions** — definitive methodological guide for clinical systematic reviews
- **PROSPERO registration** — international prospective register of systematic reviews; pre-registration is standard practice and some tools support direct export
- **GRADE (Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development, and Evaluations)** — framework for rating certainty of evidence, increasingly supported by extraction tools
- **AMSTAR-2** — critical appraisal tool for systematic reviews used to assess review quality
- **CEE Guidelines for Systematic Reviews in Environmental Management** — Collaboration for Environmental Evidence standards for non-clinical systematic reviews
- **EQUATOR Network Reporting Guidelines** — broader network of reporting standards (CONSORT, STROBE, etc.) applicable to evidence extraction

## Market Research

**Market Size:** The global systematic review and meta-analysis services and software market is estimated at USD 800 million to USD 1.5 billion, growing at approximately 12–18% CAGR driven by the exponential growth in published research and the need for evidence-based decision-making in healthcare, policy, and regulation.

**Funding:** Elicit (Ought Inc.) raised approximately $18.6 million before converting to a public benefit corporation model. Nested Knowledge (AutoLit) raised seed funding. LASER AI has institutional and government backing. Covidence is operated by a non-profit trust with Cochrane partnership. Most tools are early-stage or non-profit-supported.

**Pricing Landscape:** Free tools (Abstrackr, CADIMA, Colandr) and freemium platforms (Elicit, Rayyan) serve academic researchers. Covidence charges $55–$295/mo per review depending on team size. Enterprise and health technology assessment platforms (LASER AI, AutoLit) are custom-priced, typically $10,000–$50,000+/yr for institutional access.

**Key Buyer Personas:** Clinical and health researchers conducting Cochrane-style reviews; health technology assessment (HTA) teams at NICE, CADTH, and IQWiG; evidence synthesis researchers in environmental, social, and policy sciences; regulatory affairs teams at pharmaceutical and medical device companies; academics fulfilling journal systematic review requirements.

**Notable Trends:** AI is reducing systematic review completion time by 30–80% depending on the task. Human-in-the-loop validation remains essential for high-stakes clinical and regulatory reviews. The field is rapidly standardising around PRISMA 2020 as a benchmark for AI-assisted review reporting. Automated PRISMA flowchart generation and GRADE table population are emerging as standard AI-assisted features.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- Active learning screening engine that continuously updates document relevance predictions as reviewers make inclusion/exclusion decisions, reaching confident recommendations with fewer human screens than static classifiers
- Automated structured data extraction from PDFs, including values in tables, figures, and embedded supplementary files, mapped directly to user-defined extraction templates
- Semantic deduplication that identifies duplicate records across databases with different titles, author orderings, or publication formats, far exceeding keyword-based PMID matching
- Evidence gap mapping that visualises the distribution of included studies across population, intervention, comparator, and outcome (PICO) dimensions, highlighting under-researched areas for funders and researchers
- Living review automation that monitors new publications in configured search strings and alerts review teams when new records meet inclusion criteria, enabling continuously updated evidence syntheses without full restarts
